## Brand Positioning

Lumina's positioning is built on a single insight: analytics tools have failed the builders who need them most. They're either too complex (Amplitude, Mixpanel) or too generic (Tableau, Looker). Lumina fills the gap.

**Positioning statement:**

> "For SaaS teams who need to understand growth without drowning in data, Lumina is the analytics platform that surfaces what matters — when it matters."

This positioning is grounded in three pillars:

| Pillar | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| Speed to insight | Time-to-first-insight under 10 minutes, no data team required |
| SaaS-native | Pre-built MRR, churn, cohort, and activation metrics out of the box |
| Right-sized | Not enterprise bloat — built for teams of 10 to 150 |

Lumina does not compete on feature count. It competes on *clarity of outcome*: knowing what is driving your MRR, without hiring an analyst.

## Target Audience

Lumina's primary audience is SaaS product managers and growth leads at seed-to-Series B companies with 10–150 employees.

### Primary Persona: "The Scrappy PM"

- **Name:** Jordan, Product Manager
- **Company size:** 20–80 employees, Series A
- **Tools they use:** Notion, Linear, Slack, Segment, Stripe
- **Pain:** Spends 3+ hours weekly pulling data from Stripe + Segment into spreadsheets to answer "what's our activation rate?"
- **Goal:** Understand which features drive retention so they can prioritize roadmap
- **Budget authority:** Can approve <$500/mo without finance approval

### Secondary Persona: "The Growth Lead"

- **Name:** Alex, Head of Growth
- **Company size:** 50–150 employees, Series B
- **Pain:** Marketing attribution is a mess; can't connect acquisition channel to LTV
- **Goal:** Prove that paid acquisition is (or isn't) worth continuing

### Audience Size

- Addressable audience in North America: ~85,000 PMs and growth leads at SaaS companies
- International (English-speaking): ~140,000 additional
- Total serviceable addressable audience: ~225,000 decision-makers

## Voice & Tone

Lumina's brand voice has four defining qualities. These apply across all written surfaces: product copy, marketing, sales, support, and social.

### Voice Dimensions

| Quality | Definition | Example (Do) | Example (Don't) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Clear | No jargon, no hedging | "See which features drive retention" | "Leverage actionable cohort intelligence" |
| Confident | Data-backed, no fluff | "Teams using Lumina cut reporting time by 70%" | "We believe data can transform your business" |
| Human | Written for people, not search engines | "You're losing users at step 3. Here's why." | "Our platform facilitates user lifecycle analysis" |
| Focused | Outcomes over features | "Know your MRR drivers in 10 minutes" | "Lumina includes 47 pre-built report templates" |

### Tone by Context

- **Marketing:** Confident and direct. Lead with the problem, land on the outcome.
- **Product UI:** Clear and minimal. Every label earns its place.
- **Error messages:** Human and helpful. Tell the user what happened and what to do.
- **Support:** Warm and knowledgeable. Assume good intent; solve the problem.
- **Sales:** Consultative. Ask questions before pitching.

## Differentiation

Lumina is positioned against three primary competitors. Each comparison reinforces a distinct element of the Lumina value proposition.

### Competitive Matrix

| Competitor | Their Strength | Their Weakness | Lumina's Angle |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| Mixpanel | Event tracking depth | Complex setup; requires analyst | Simpler + pre-built SaaS metrics |
| Amplitude | Behavioral analytics | High price; long implementation | Lower price + faster time-to-insight |
| Tableau | Visualization power | Requires data team + SQL | No data team required |
| Baremetrics | MRR/revenue metrics | Revenue only; no product data | Full-funnel: acquisition → revenue |

### Differentiation Statement

Lumina is the only analytics platform designed specifically for early-to-mid-stage SaaS teams — with SaaS-native metrics built in, a 10-minute time-to-insight guarantee, and pricing that doesn't require a VP signature.

### Proof Points

- **Speed:** Median time from signup to first meaningful insight: 8 minutes
- **Adoption:** 82% of users return within 48 hours of first session (vs. industry avg of 38%)
- **Simplicity:** Zero SQL queries required to access all pre-built reports

## Brand Value Proposition

Lumina's value proposition is written for three audiences: investors, buyers, and users.

### For Investors

> "Lumina is the analytics layer for the SaaS middle market — a $4.2B segment underserved by enterprise tools and outgrowing point solutions."

### For Buyers (Decision-Maker)

> "See what's actually driving your MRR in under 10 minutes — without a data team."

### For Users (Day-to-Day)

> "Stop pulling reports. Start seeing answers."

### Value Hierarchy

1. **Primary value:** Speed to insight (replaces hours of manual analysis)
2. **Secondary value:** SaaS-native metrics (no custom setup for standard SaaS KPIs)
3. **Tertiary value:** Team alignment (share insights in Slack, Linear, or Notion with one click)

## Brand Story

Lumina was built by former SaaS founders who spent more time in spreadsheets than talking to customers.

In 2019, co-founders Maya and Daniel were running a B2B SaaS product with 600 customers. Every week, they'd lose Monday morning to a ritual: pulling data from Stripe, Mixpanel, and Segment into a Google Sheet, trying to answer the same questions: Who churned? What's our activation rate? Which cohort performs best?

They tried every analytics tool on the market. Mixpanel required a full-time analyst to set up. Amplitude was priced for enterprises with data teams. Tableau needed SQL. Baremetrics only covered revenue.

So they built Lumina for themselves. When they showed it to five other founders, all five asked to use it.

Today, Lumina serves 1,200+ SaaS teams across 40 countries. It has grown to $480K ARR in 18 months with no paid marketing — entirely through founder-to-founder word of mouth and a product that people actually talk about.

**The brand is built on the belief that clarity is a competitive advantage.** Teams that understand their data move faster, waste less, and build better products. Lumina exists to give every SaaS team — not just the ones that can afford data teams — that advantage.
